\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

This fixture exercises the single hardest table layout in the corpus: a booktabs
table that merges cells in both directions at once. The cross-tabulation in
\cref{tab:crosstab} arranges three row groups against two column groups, so that
column~1 carries vertical row-group labels built with \verb|\multirow| while the
header carries horizontal column-group spanners built with \verb|\multicolumn|.
The two devices interact at the top-left corner, where a stub head spans two
header rows down and two stub columns across, and they must be reconciled by any
converter that hopes to reproduce the grid.

The header has three physical rows. The first row places two top-level spanners,
``Pre-Reform'' across three estimate columns and ``Post-Reform'' across two, each
underlined by a partial rule drawn with \verb|\cmidrule(lr)| so that the rule
governs only its own block rather than the full table width. The second header
row subdivides the pre-reform block with a nested \verb|\multicolumn| spanner,
``By Tercile,'' that covers two of its three columns, leaving the third column
heading free-standing. The third header row names the individual columns. A
converter that flattens these levels will collapse the nested spanner into the
column names and lose both \verb|\cmidrule| spans.

\section{Cross-Tabulation}
\label{sec:crosstab}

\Cref{tab:crosstab} reports synthetic coefficients for eight specifications,
organized as three row groups, namely Size, Liquidity, and a single pooled
summary row that itself spans the two stub columns with a \verb|\multicolumn|.
The row groups labelled Size and Liquidity each span three and two body rows
respectively, so the \verb|\multirow| labels sit vertically centred against
their member rows. The interaction estimand
$\text{Treated}\times\text{Post}$ is stable across the pre- and post-reform
blocks at roughly $\widehat{\beta}\approx 0.10$.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The substantive reading of \cref{tab:crosstab} is that the interaction effect
attenuates monotonically with firm size and with market depth, while remaining
materially unchanged between the pre- and post-reform windows. For a fidelity
test, however, the layout is the object of interest. Three failure modes are
combined deliberately in one table rather than tested in isolation: a converter
that drops the vertical \verb|\multirow| labels will leave the Size and Liquidity
groups unlabelled against their member rows; one that flattens the stacked
\verb|\multicolumn| header levels will merge the nested ``By Tercile'' spanner
into the column names and discard the \verb|\cmidrule| partial rules; and one
that ignores the trailing two-column summary row will lose the pooled estimates
that anchor the bottom of the grid. Each is a distinct defect, and a faithful
rendering must survive all three at once.
