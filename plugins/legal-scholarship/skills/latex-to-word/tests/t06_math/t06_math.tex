Inline mathematics such as $x_i \in \mathbb{R}$ and $\alpha+\beta=1$ should
remain editable after conversion. The display equation in \eqref{eq:loss}
provides a label for later cross-reference.

\begin{align}
L(\theta)
  &= \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left(y_i - x_i^{\top}\theta\right)^2
     + \lambda \lVert \theta \rVert_2^2 \label{eq:loss}\\
\frac{\partial L}{\partial \theta}
  &= -2X^{\top}(y-X\theta)+2\lambda\theta.
\end{align}

The decision rule can be written as a cases expression:
\[
d(x)=
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if } x^{\top}\theta \geq \tau,\\
0, & \text{if } x^{\top}\theta < \tau.
\end{cases}
\]

The same notation appears in a small matrix:
\[
M =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & \rho & 0\\
\rho & 1 & \rho\\
0 & \rho & 1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

\begin{theorem}
\label{thm:ridge}
If $X^{\top}X+\lambda I$ is nonsingular and $\lambda>0$, the minimizer of
\eqref{eq:loss} is unique.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Hessian of $L(\theta)$ is $2X^{\top}X+2\lambda I$. For any nonzero vector
$v$, the quadratic form is
\[
v^{\top}(2X^{\top}X+2\lambda I)v = 2\lVert Xv\rVert_2^2+2\lambda\lVert v\rVert_2^2,
\]
which is strictly positive because $\lambda>0$. Strict convexity gives a unique
minimizer.
\end{proof}
