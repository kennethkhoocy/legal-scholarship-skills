\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

Under the assumptions stated in \cref{sec:methods}, with the parameter values
listed in \cref{tab:params}, the ordinary least squares estimator
$\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}$ has the sampling variance given in \cref{eq:variance}.
The expression depends only on the noise scale $\sigma$ and the fixed design
matrix $X$, so substituting the tabulated value of $\sigma$ yields a numerical
covariance directly.

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:variance}
\operatorname{Var}\bigl(\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}\bigr)
  = \sigma^{2}\,\bigl(X^{\top} X\bigr)^{-1}.
\end{equation}

Two features of \cref{eq:variance} are worth noting. First, the variance scales
with $\sigma^{2}$, so the disturbance scale of $0.10$ from \cref{tab:params}
enters quadratically and the resulting standard errors are small. Second, the
estimator's precision is governed entirely by the Gram matrix $X^{\top} X$;
holding $\sigma$ fixed, a better-conditioned design tightens every coefficient
simultaneously. Together these observations close the argument begun in
\cref{sec:intro}, demonstrating that the cross-file references resolve into a
single coherent line of reasoning.
