\begin{abstract}
This fixture tests a wide table wrapped in \texttt{\textbackslash resizebox} at
\texttt{\textbackslash textwidth}. The table uses booktabs rules and many columns
to emulate a compact empirical reporting table.
\end{abstract}

\section{Wide Descriptive Table}
\label{sec:wide-table}

Applied papers often compress many related estimates into a single table. The
source below uses a resize box because the raw tabular width exceeds the normal
line width.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Wide synthetic site-period summary}
\label{tab:wide-summary}
\resizebox{\textwidth}{!}{%
\begin{tabular}{lrrrrrrrrr}
\toprule
Site type & Cases & Baseline & Follow-up & Change & Missing & Reviews & Appeals & Staff & Score \\
\midrule
Urban primary & 184 & 0.42 & 0.51 & 0.09 & 0.03 & 27 & 5 & 12 & 81.4 \\
Urban referral & 126 & 0.39 & 0.47 & 0.08 & 0.05 & 31 & 7 & 9 & 76.2 \\
Suburban primary & 143 & 0.44 & 0.49 & 0.05 & 0.04 & 19 & 4 & 8 & 78.6 \\
Suburban referral & 88 & 0.37 & 0.45 & 0.08 & 0.06 & 22 & 6 & 6 & 72.9 \\
Rural primary & 97 & 0.36 & 0.43 & 0.07 & 0.07 & 18 & 3 & 5 & 69.8 \\
Rural referral & 64 & 0.33 & 0.40 & 0.07 & 0.08 & 16 & 4 & 4 & 65.1 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}%
}
\end{table}

The conversion target should retain the table as a coherent object rather than
dropping columns or expanding it beyond the page margins. Exact scaling may vary
in Word, although all columns and booktabs rules should remain visible.
