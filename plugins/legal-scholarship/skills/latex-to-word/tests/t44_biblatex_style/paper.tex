\section{Citation Commands}
\label{sec:citations}

This fixture exercises the principal citation commands that a biblatex document
exposes when the author-year style is paired with the biber backend, so that a
conversion path must resolve each command to its rendered form and assemble the
reference list from the embedded database. The methodological turn toward
research designs with credible identification is described by \textcite{angrist2010},
whose argument frames much of the applied work that follows. Standard panel-data
estimators and their assumptions are treated at length in a canonical reference
\parencite{wooldridge2010}, and a specific caution about serial correlation in
the difference-in-differences setting appears at \parencite[15]{bertrand2004},
where the page-prefixed form points the reader to the relevant discussion.

Inference under clustering supplies a second cluster of references. Multiway
clustering is developed by \autocite{cameron2011}, and a broad textbook account
of the surrounding estimation theory is available as well \autocite{greene2018}.
When several sources speak to the same point, a multi-key parenthetical collects
them in a single call \parencite{wooldridge2010,greene2018}, which the
author-year style sorts and compresses according to its own rules. \Cref{sec:citations}
therefore combines textual, parenthetical, page-qualified, and multi-key forms in
adjacent sentences, so that any one of them failing to resolve would be visible
in the rendered output.
