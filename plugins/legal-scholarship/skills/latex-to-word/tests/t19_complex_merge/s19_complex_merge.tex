\begin{abstract}
This fixture combines row spans, grouped column headers, partial rules, and
multicolumn summary cells. The table is designed to exercise merged-cell
semantics in a standard academic reporting layout.
\end{abstract}

\section{Merged-Cell Layout}
\label{sec:merged-layout}

The table reports synthetic baseline and follow-up measures by cohort and study
arm. Header groups use \texttt{\textbackslash multicolumn} with
\texttt{\textbackslash cmidrule}, while cohort labels use
\texttt{\textbackslash multirow} across treatment arms.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Synthetic outcomes with row and column merges}
\label{tab:complex-merge}
\begin{tabular}{llrrrrrr}

\cmidrule(lr){3-5}\cmidrule(lr){6-8}
 &  & $N$ & Mean & S.D. & $N$ & Mean & S.D. \\

\multirow{2}{*}{2019}
  & Control & 82 & 0.41 & 0.18 & 79 & 0.44 & 0.19 \\
  & Treated & 86 & 0.42 & 0.17 & 84 & 0.53 & 0.18 \\

  & Treated & 94 & 0.40 & 0.19 & 92 & 0.52 & 0.20 \\

\multicolumn{2}{l}{Pooled treated-control difference}
  & \multicolumn{3}{c}{0.01} & \multicolumn{3}{c}{0.09} \\

Table~\ref{tab:complex-merge} creates genuine merged cells in both directions.
The final row also spans several columns, which gives later conversion checks a
clear target for summary rows.
